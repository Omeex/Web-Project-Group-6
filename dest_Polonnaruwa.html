Polonnaruwa Ancient City

Polonnaruwa is an ancient city in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka and one of the country's most important archaeological sites. It served as the second capital of Sri Lanka after Anuradhapura and reached its golden age during the reign of King Parakramabahu I (1153–1186 CE).

Today, Polonnaruwa is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, celebrated for its well-preserved ruins, impressive stone carvings, and sophisticated ancient irrigation systems that reflect the advanced engineering skills of early Sri Lankan civilization.

History

Polonnaruwa became the capital of Sri Lanka in the 11th century following the decline of Anuradhapura. The city reached its peak during the reign of King Parakramabahu I (1153–1186 CE), who transformed it into a thriving urban center with grand buildings and vast reservoirs. After a series of foreign invasions, Polonnaruwa was gradually abandoned and remained hidden in the jungle for centuries before being rediscovered and restored.

What to See & Do

  • Royal Palace: Explore the ruins of King Parakramabahu's palace, once believed to have seven stories and over 1,000 rooms.
  • Gal Vihara: Visit the iconic rock temple featuring four magnificent Buddha statues carved from a single granite rock face.
  • Sacred Quadrangle (Dalada Maluwa): A cluster of ancient buildings including the Vatadage, Thuparama, and Hatadage.
  • Parakrama Samudra: Walk or relax beside the vast man-made reservoir built to supply water to the city, a masterpiece of ancient hydraulic engineering.
  • Lankatilaka & Tivanka Shrines: Admire towering brick temples known for their architectural beauty and surviving murals.
  • Archaeological Museum: Learn about Polonnaruwa's history through artifacts and exhibits.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Polonnaruwa is early morning or late afternoon, when temperatures are cooler and lighting is ideal for sightseeing. The dry season from December to April is most suitable, while May to September tends to be less crowded. The archaeological park usually opens at 7:00 AM.

Travel Tips

  • Wear comfortable walking shoes with good grip
  • Carry plenty of water (at least 1–2 liters per person)
  • Respect religious sites by removing shoes and covering shoulders and knees
  • Don't forget sunscreen and a hat
  • Renting a bicycle is the easiest way to explore the site
  • Hiring a local guide provides deeper historical insight
