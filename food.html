Sri Lankan Cuisine

Discover the bold flavors and aromatic spices that make Sri Lankan food unforgettable.

Back to Home

Must-Try Sri Lankan Food

Rice & Curry

The cornerstone of Sri Lankan cuisine. A typical meal includes rice served with several different curries, sambols, and accompaniments.

Main Curries

  • Chicken Curry: Spicy curry with coconut milk
  • Fish Curry: Often made with tuna or seer fish
  • Dhal Curry: Lentil curry, a staple at every meal
  • Potato Curry: Mild curry popular with everyone
  • Jackfruit Curry: Vegetarian option when in season

Sambols & Accompaniments

  • Pol Sambol: Spicy coconut relish
  • Gotu Kola Sambol: Herb and coconut salad
  • Papadam: Crispy lentil crackers
  • Pickles: Various fruit and vegetable pickles

Street Food & Snacks

Sri Lanka has a vibrant street food culture with delicious and affordable options available throughout the day.

Hoppers (Appa)

Bowl-shaped pancakes made from fermented rice flour and coconut milk. Often served with an egg in the center (egg hopper).

Kottu Roti

Chopped roti stir-fried with vegetables, egg, and/or meat, cooked on a hot griddle with rhythmic clacking sounds.

Short Eats

Various savory pastries and snacks like samosas, rolls, and patties filled with meat, fish, or vegetables.

Seafood

With its long coastline, Sri Lanka offers excellent seafood, especially in coastal areas like Negombo, Galle, and Trincomalee.

Popular Seafood Dishes

  • Ambul Thiyal: Sour fish curry unique to southern Sri Lanka
  • Prawn Curry: Fresh prawns in spicy coconut gravy
  • Crab Curry: Especially popular in Negombo
  • Fried Sprats: Small fish fried until crispy
  • Lobster: Grilled or in curry, a coastal delicacy

Where to Find

  • Negombo: Known as "Little Rome" for its seafood
  • Galle: Beachfront restaurants with fresh catch
  • Trincomalee: Especially good for crab
  • Local Markets: For the freshest selection

Desserts & Sweets

Sri Lankan desserts often feature coconut, jaggery (palm sugar), rice flour, and treacle.

Traditional Sweets

Kiribath

Milk rice cooked with coconut milk, traditionally served for breakfast on special occasions and festivals.

Watalappan

Steamed coconut custard pudding with jaggery and spices like cardamom and nutmeg.

Kevum

Oil cakes made from rice flour and treacle, especially popular during the New Year.

Drinks

From world-famous tea to refreshing local beverages, Sri Lanka offers a variety of drinks to complement its cuisine.

Ceylon Tea

Sri Lanka is one of the world's largest tea producers. Try different varieties like Dimbula, Nuwara Eliya, and Uva.

King Coconut Water

Refreshing natural drink from king coconuts, sold by roadside vendors throughout the country.

Wood Apple Juice

Unique local fruit with a distinctive flavor, often mixed with sugar and spices.

Spices of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka has been famous for its spices since ancient times. Many dishes get their distinctive flavor from these spices.

Cinnamon

Sri Lanka produces 80-90% of the world's true cinnamon

Cardamom

Used in both savory dishes and desserts

Cloves

Adds warmth to meat dishes and curries

Pepper

Known as "black gold" in ancient times

Turmeric

Adds color and health benefits to dishes

Curry Leaves

Essential for authentic Sri Lankan flavor

Where to Eat

Local Restaurants

Small family-run establishments offering authentic home-style cooking at reasonable prices.

Hotel Buffets

Great way to sample a wide variety of Sri Lankan dishes, especially for first-time visitors.

Cooking Classes

Learn to prepare Sri Lankan dishes yourself. Available in major tourist areas.

Dietary Considerations

Vegetarian Options

Plenty of vegetarian dishes available. Many Sri Lankans eat vegetarian on poya (full moon) days.

Spice Levels

Food can be very spicy. Ask for "less spicy" if you're not accustomed to heat.

Food Safety

Stick to busy restaurants with high turnover. Drink bottled water and avoid uncooked vegetables.
