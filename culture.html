Sri Lankan Culture & Heritage

Discover the rich traditions, arts, and customs that have shaped Sri Lanka for over 2,500 years.

Back to Home

Cultural Heritage

Religious Heritage

Sri Lanka is a multi-religious country where Buddhism, Hinduism, Islam, and Christianity coexist. Buddhism has the strongest influence on Sri Lankan culture, having been introduced in the 3rd century BCE.

Buddhism

Sri Lanka is one of the world's oldest continuous Buddhist societies. The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic in Kandy houses Buddha's tooth and is one of Buddhism's most sacred sites.

Hinduism

Hinduism has a strong presence, especially in the Northern and Eastern provinces. The Nallur Kandaswamy Temple in Jaffna is one of the most significant Hindu temples.

Islam & Christianity

Muslims and Christians have contributed significantly to Sri Lanka's cultural diversity, with beautiful mosques and churches across the island.

Traditional Arts

Sri Lanka has a rich tradition of arts and crafts that have been passed down through generations.

Dance

Sri Lankan traditional dance includes Kandyan, Sabaragamuwa, and Low Country styles. Each has distinct costumes, rhythms, and movements, often telling religious or historical stories.

Music

Traditional music features instruments like the rabana (drum), thammattama (twin drum), and horanava (oboe). Baila music shows Portuguese influence.

Handicrafts

Wood carving, mask making, lacquer work, and handloom textiles are traditional crafts. Ambalangoda is famous for its intricate wooden masks.

Architecture

Sri Lanka's architecture reflects its diverse history, from ancient Buddhist stupas to colonial buildings.

Ancient Architecture

Stupas, palaces, and monastic complexes from Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa, and Sigiriya periods.

Colonial Architecture

Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences visible in forts, churches, and government buildings.

Traditional Architecture

Vernacular styles using local materials like wood, clay, and thatch, adapted to tropical climate.

Festivals

Sri Lanka celebrates numerous festivals throughout the year, reflecting its multi-ethnic and multi-religious society.

Major Festivals

  • Vesak (May): Celebrates Buddha's birth, enlightenment, and passing. Streets are decorated with lanterns and pandals.
  • Esala Perahera (July/August): Kandy's spectacular procession with dancers, drummers, and decorated elephants.
  • Thai Pongal (January): Hindu harvest festival giving thanks to the sun god.
  • Ramadan & Eid: Muslim holy month followed by festive celebrations.
  • Christmas (December): Celebrated by Christians across the island.
  • Sinhala & Tamil New Year (April): The most important national festival with traditional games, food, and rituals.

Customs & Etiquette

Understanding local customs will enhance your experience in Sri Lanka and show respect for the culture.

Greetings

The traditional greeting is "Ayubowan" (may you live long) with palms pressed together. Handshakes are common in business settings.

Temple Etiquette

Remove shoes and hats before entering religious sites. Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered). Don't pose with your back to Buddha statues.

General Etiquette

Use your right hand for eating and giving/receiving items. Avoid touching people's heads. Public displays of affection are frowned upon.

UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Sri Lanka has eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites that showcase its rich cultural and natural heritage.

  • Ancient City of Polonnaruwa - Medieval capital with well-preserved ruins
  • Ancient City of Sigiriya - 5th-century rock fortress with frescoes
  • Sacred City of Anuradhapura - Ancient capital and Buddhist pilgrimage site
  • Sacred City of Kandy - Last royal capital and home to the Temple of the Tooth
  • Old Town of Galle and its Fortifications - Best-preserved colonial fort in Asia
  • Golden Temple of Dambulla - Cave temple complex with Buddha statues
  • Sinharaja Forest Reserve - Biodiversity hotspot with endemic species
  • Central Highlands of Sri Lanka - Protected area including Horton Plains and Peak Wilderness
