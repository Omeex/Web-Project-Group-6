Anuradhapura Ancient City

The first ancient capital of Sri Lanka and a UNESCO World Heritage Site

About Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura, founded in the 4th century BC, was the first ancient capital of Sri Lanka and remained the royal seat for over 1,300 years. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and a major Buddhist pilgrimage site.

The city spans over 40 square kilometers and contains some of the most impressive Buddhist monuments in Asia, including enormous stupas (dagobas), ancient reservoirs, and monastic complexes. Anuradhapura represents the golden age of Sinhalese civilization and Buddhist architecture.

History

Anuradhapura was established by King Pandukabhaya in 377 BC and flourished as the capital until 1017 AD. The city reached its peak during the reign of King Dutugemunu (161-137 BC), who constructed many of the monumental structures that still stand today.

The city was abandoned after repeated South Indian invasions and was eventually reclaimed by the jungle. It was rediscovered by British explorers in the 19th century and has since been meticulously restored and preserved as one of the world's most important archaeological sites.

What to See & Do

  • Sri Maha Bodhi: Visit the sacred Bodhi tree grown from a cutting of the original tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment, over 2,300 years old.
  • Ruwanwelisaya: Marvel at this magnificent white stupa built by King Dutugemunu, standing 103 meters high with a circumference of 290 meters.
  • Jetavanaramaya: See one of the tallest ancient structures in the world, originally 122 meters high, built entirely of bricks.
  • Abhayagiri Monastery: Explore the ruins of this massive monastic complex that once housed 5,000 monks.
  • Samadhi Buddha Statue: Admire this exquisite 4th-century granite statue of Buddha in meditation pose, considered one of the finest Buddha statues in Sri Lanka.
  • Isurumuniya Rock Temple: Visit this rock temple famous for its beautiful carvings including the "Lovers of Isurumuniya."
  • Kuttam Pokuna (Twin Ponds): See the exquisite ancient bathing ponds with sophisticated water filtration systems.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Anuradhapura is during the dry season from May to September. The site is open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Early morning (6:00 AM - 9:00 AM) is ideal to avoid the heat and experience the sites during cooler temperatures. The Poson Poya festival in June attracts thousands of pilgrims and offers a unique cultural experience.

Travel Tips

  • Rent a bicycle or hire a tuk-tuk to cover the large area
  • Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered) for temple visits
  • Remove shoes and hats when entering sacred sites
  • Carry plenty of water and wear sunscreen
  • Visit the Archaeological Museum first for context
  • Hire a licensed guide for detailed historical information
