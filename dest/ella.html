Ella

Mountain paradise with stunning views, waterfalls, and tea plantations

About Ella

Ella is a picturesque small town in the Badulla District of Sri Lanka's Uva Province, nestled in the central highlands at an elevation of 1,041 meters (3,415 feet). Known for its cool climate, breathtaking mountain views, and lush tea plantations, Ella has become one of Sri Lanka's most popular hill country destinations.

The town offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, adventure activities, and relaxation. From hiking to stunning viewpoints and exploring waterfalls to enjoying freshly brewed Ceylon tea, Ella provides an unforgettable experience for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

History

Ella's modern development began with the arrival of British tea planters in the 19th century who established tea plantations in the surrounding hills. The town's name is believed to derive from the Sinhalese word "Ella," meaning waterfall, referring to the numerous waterfalls in the area.

The construction of the Colombo-Badulla railway line in the early 20th century made Ella more accessible and contributed to its growth. Today, Ella has transformed from a quiet hill station to a vibrant tourist destination while maintaining its natural charm and beauty.

What to See & Do

  • Ella Rock: Hike to this popular viewpoint for panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The 8km round-trip takes 3-4 hours.
  • Little Adam's Peak: Enjoy an easier hike to this beautiful viewpoint with stunning tea plantation vistas, especially magical at sunrise.
  • Nine Arches Bridge: Marvel at this iconic colonial-era railway bridge built entirely of brick, stone, and cement without any steel.
  • Ravana Falls: Visit one of Sri Lanka's widest waterfalls, named after the legendary king Ravana from the Ramayana epic.
  • Tea Plantation Tours: Visit a working tea factory like Uva Halpewaththa Tea Factory to learn about tea production and enjoy fresh Ceylon tea.
  • Ravana's Cave: Explore this archaeological and historical site believed to be where King Ravana imprisoned Princess Sita.
  • Train Journey: Experience one of the world's most scenic train rides from Ella to Kandy or Nuwara Eliya.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Ella is from January to March and from July to September when the weather is dry and pleasant. The temperature ranges from 15°C to 25°C year-round. December to February offers the clearest skies for hiking and photography. Avoid the monsoon months of October and November when heavy rains can disrupt outdoor activities.

Travel Tips

  • Wear sturdy hiking shoes with good grip for Ella Rock and Little Adam's Peak
  • Carry a light jacket as evenings can be cool
  • Start hikes early in the morning for the best weather and views
  • Book train tickets in advance during peak season
  • Try local rice and curry at authentic Sri Lankan restaurants
  • Stay in a guesthouse with mountain views for the full experience
