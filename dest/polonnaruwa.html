Polonnaruwa Ancient City

Medieval capital of Sri Lanka with magnificent ruins and ancient irrigation systems

About Polonnaruwa

Polonnaruwa is the second most ancient kingdom of Sri Lanka, serving as the capital from the 11th to the 13th century. This UNESCO World Heritage Site features remarkably well-preserved ruins of ancient gardens, monuments, palaces, and Buddhist temples.

The city showcases the architectural and engineering prowess of ancient Sri Lankan civilization, with sophisticated irrigation systems that still function today. The archaeological park spans over 122 hectares and offers a fascinating glimpse into medieval Sinhalese culture.

History

Polonnaruwa succeeded Anuradhapura as the capital in the late 10th century. It reached its zenith under King Parakramabahu I (1153-1186 AD), who unified the country and undertook massive construction projects. The city was meticulously planned with separate quarters for royalty, merchants, and religious orders, and featured an extensive network of parks, gardens, and irrigation tanks.

After the decline of the Polonnaruwa kingdom in the 13th century, the capital shifted again, and the city was gradually reclaimed by the jungle until its archaeological rediscovery in the early 20th century.

What to See & Do

  • Royal Palace Complex: Explore the ruins of King Parakramabahu's seven-story palace, council chambers, and royal baths.
  • Quadrangle (Dalada Maluwa): The sacred precinct containing the Vatadage (circular relic house), Thuparama (image house), and other important religious structures.
  • Gal Vihara: Marvel at the magnificent rock temple with four Buddha statues carved from a single granite rock face, including a 14-meter reclining Buddha.
  • Parakrama Samudra: Visit the massive man-made reservoir (sea of Parakrama) built by King Parakramabahu I, covering 2,400 hectares.
  • Archaeological Museum: Discover artifacts, models, and information about Polonnaruwa's history and excavations.
  • Lankatilaka: See the impressive brick-walled image house with a standing Buddha statue that was originally 17 meters tall.
  • Rankoth Vehera: Climb the largest stupa in Polonnaruwa, standing 55 meters high and resembling the stupas of Anuradhapura.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Polonnaruwa is during the dry season from May to September when rainfall is minimal. Early morning (7:00 AM - 10:00 AM) is ideal to avoid the midday heat. The site is open from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM daily. December to March is also pleasant, though this is peak tourist season.

Travel Tips

  • Rent a bicycle to explore the expansive site comfortably
  • Hire a knowledgeable local guide for historical insights
  • Carry water, sunscreen, and a hat as shade is limited
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes
  • Visit the museum first for context before exploring ruins
  • Respect religious sites - dress modestly and remove shoes
