Essential Travel Information

Best Time to Visit

Sri Lanka has two main monsoon seasons, which affect different parts of the island at different times.

West & South Coasts

Best: December to March
Monsoon: May to September
Includes Colombo, Galle, Mirissa, Bentota

East Coast

Best: April to September
Monsoon: November to February
Includes Trincomalee, Arugam Bay, Batticaloa

Hill Country

Best: January to March
Rainy: May to July, October to November
Includes Kandy, Nuwara Eliya, Ella

Peak Seasons

  • December to March: Peak tourist season with best weather on west/south coasts
  • July & August: European summer holidays, busy throughout
  • Shoulder Seasons (April, September-October): Good weather with fewer crowds

Getting Around Sri Lanka

Trains

Scenic and affordable. Book 1st or 2nd class reserved seats in advance, especially for popular routes like Kandy-Ella.

Buses

Extensive network covering entire island. Cheap but can be crowded. Private buses are faster than government buses.

Tuk-tuks

Three-wheelers ideal for short distances. Always negotiate the price before getting in. Use meter tuk-tuks when available.

Other Transport Options

  • Private Driver: Most comfortable option for touring. Costs $50-80 per day including fuel.
  • Domestic Flights: Available between Colombo, Jaffna, Trincomalee, and other cities.
  • Car Rental: Can be challenging due to left-hand driving and chaotic traffic. Driver recommended.

Accommodation

Budget

Guesthouses: Family-run accommodations, $15-40 per night
Hostels: Dorm beds $8-15, private rooms $20-30
Best for: Backpackers, solo travelers

Mid-range

Boutique Hotels: $50-150 per night
Villas: Private villas with pools, $80-200
Best for: Couples, families

Luxury

Resorts: $150-500+ per night
Eco Lodges: Sustainable luxury in nature
Best for: Honeymooners, luxury travelers

Booking Tips

  • Book well in advance during peak season (Dec-Mar, Jul-Aug)
  • Check multiple booking sites for best prices
  • Read recent reviews before booking
  • Consider location - being central can save transportation costs
  • Many places offer airport pickup for additional fee

Money & Costs

Currency

Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR)
Current rate: ~1 USD = 320 LKR (check before travel)
Bring newer US dollars (post-2009) for best exchange rates

Budgeting

Budget: $30-50 per day
Mid-range: $50-100 per day
Luxury: $150+ per day
Prices are per person excluding flights

Payment Methods

Cash is king, especially outside major cities
Credit cards accepted at hotels and larger restaurants
ATMs widely available in cities and tourist areas

Health & Safety

Vaccinations

  • Routine vaccines up to date
  • Hepatitis A & Typhoid recommended
  • Consider Hepatitis B, Rabies, Japanese Encephalitis
  • Malaria pills not usually needed for tourist areas

Health Precautions

  • Drink bottled or boiled water
  • Use insect repellent, especially at dusk
  • Pack diarrhea medication
  • Use sunscreen - sun is strong

Safety Tips

  • Sri Lanka is generally safe for tourists
  • Be cautious of scams in tourist areas
  • Keep valuables secure
  • Respect local customs and dress codes

What to Pack

Clothing

  • Light, breathable clothing
  • Modest attire for temple visits (cover shoulders/knees)
  • Swimwear and sarong/cover-up
  • Light rain jacket
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sandals/flip-flops

Essentials

  • Passport with 6 months validity
  • Photocopies of important documents
  • Travel insurance details
  • Power adapter (Type D, M, or G plugs)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Daypack for excursions

Health & Toiletries

  • Insect repellent with DEET
  • Sunscreen (high SPF)
  • Basic first aid kit
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Any prescription medications
  • Anti-diarrhea medication

Cultural Etiquette

Temples & Religious Sites

  • Remove shoes and hats
  • Dress modestly (shoulders/knees covered)
  • Don't turn your back to Buddha statues for photos
  • Don't touch monks if you're female
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people

General Etiquette

  • Use your right hand for eating and giving/receiving
  • Avoid touching people's heads
  • Public displays of affection are frowned upon
  • Remove shoes before entering homes
  • Address elders with respect

Photography

  • Always ask permission before photographing people
  • Some sites charge camera fees in addition to entry fees
  • No photography allowed at some religious ceremonies
  • Be discreet when taking photos in sensitive areas

Visa & Entry Requirements

Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA)

  • Most nationalities need an ETA before arrival
  • Apply online at www.eta.gov.lk
  • Cost: $50 USD for 30-day tourist visa
  • Processing: Usually within 24 hours
  • Double entry visa also available

Requirements

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Sufficient funds for your stay
  • Confirmed accommodation (may be asked)

On Arrival

  • ETA can be obtained on arrival but costs more and involves waiting
  • Recommended to apply online before travel
  • Visa extensions possible at Department of Immigration in Colombo
