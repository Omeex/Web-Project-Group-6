Sinharaja Forest Reserve

UNESCO World Heritage Site and Sri Lanka's last viable rainforest

About Sinharaja

Sinharaja Forest Reserve is a national park and a biodiversity hotspot in Sri Lanka. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and International Man and Biosphere Reserve, recognized for its exceptional biodiversity and unique ecosystem.

The forest reserve covers an area of 8,864 hectares and is the country's last viable area of primary tropical rainforest. The name "Sinharaja" means "Lion King" and reflects its historical significance as a royal forest.

Biodiversity

Sinharaja is home to over 60% of Sri Lanka's endemic species. The forest contains a remarkable variety of flora and fauna, including rare birds, mammals, reptiles, amphibians, and countless insect species. More than 50% of Sri Lanka's endemic butterflies and birds are found here.

What to See & Do

  • Bird Watching: Spot endemic birds like Sri Lanka Blue Magpie, Red-faced Malkoha, and Sri Lanka Spurfowl.
  • Nature Trails: Explore well-marked trails including Sinhagala trail, Mulawella trail, and the research trail.
  • Waterfalls: Discover hidden waterfalls like Duwili Ella and Pathan Oya within the forest.
  • Flora Discovery: See endemic plants including the rare palm Loxococcus rupicola and numerous medicinal plants.
  • Butterfly Watching: Observe rare butterfly species including the spectacular Birdwing butterflies.
  • Research Center: Visit the Sinharaja Rainforest Research Center to learn about conservation efforts.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Sinharaja is during the dry season from December to April and from July to September. Early morning (6:00 AM - 9:00 AM) is ideal for bird watching and wildlife spotting. Avoid the heavy monsoon months of May-June and October-November when trails can be slippery and leeches are more active.

Travel Tips

  • Hire a certified guide - it's mandatory and enhances the experience
  • Wear leech socks (available for rent at entry points)
  • Dress in light, breathable clothing in earthy colors
  • Carry waterproof bags for electronics and important items
  • Bring insect repellent and plenty of drinking water
  • Stay on marked trails for safety and conservation

What to Bring

  • Sturdy hiking shoes with good grip
  • Rain jacket or poncho
  • Binoculars for bird watching
  • Camera with protective case
  • Energy snacks
  • First aid kit including antiseptic
