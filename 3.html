Accountancy

Accountancy is the practice of recording, classifying, and reporting on business transactions for a business. It provides feedback to management regarding the financial results and status of an organization.

NOTES

    Career Paths

    Accountants can work in public practice, industry, government, or education. Roles include auditor, tax advisor, financial analyst, and management accountant.

    Economics

    Economics is the social science that studies how people interact with value; in particular, the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. Economics focuses on the behavior and interactions of economic agents and how economies work.

    Key Concepts

    • Supply and Demand
    • Market Structures
    • Macroeconomic Indicators (GDP, Inflation, Unemployment)
    • Monetary and Fiscal Policy
    • International Trade

    Career Paths

    Economists work in government agencies, research institutions, international organizations, financial institutions, and consulting firms.

    Business Studies

    Business Studies is an academic subject that combines elements of accountancy, finance, marketing, organizational studies, human resource management, and operations in the context of a business or enterprise.

    Key Concepts

    • Business Organization and Environment
    • Human Resource Management
    • Marketing and Market Research
    • Operations Management
    • Business Strategy

    Career Paths

    Graduates can pursue careers in management, marketing, human resources, entrepreneurship, and business consultancy across various industries.

    Information & Communication Technology

    ICT focuses on the use of technology to manage and process information. This includes software applications, computer systems and networks, databases, and telecommunications systems that enable users to access, store, transmit, and manipulate information.

    Key Concepts

    • Computer Systems and Architecture
    • Programming and Software Development
    • Database Management Systems
    • Networking and Cybersecurity
    • Web Technologies

    Career Paths

    ICT professionals work as software developers, network administrators, data analysts, cybersecurity specialists, IT consultants, and systems architects.
